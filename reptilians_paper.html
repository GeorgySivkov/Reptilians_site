How it's gonna roll

Overview

Interaction based on NFTs is at the core of the Reptilians' mechanics. They are used as a way for community members to have a say in the development of the Reptilians' products and services. Community members can purchase or receive NFTs through marketing promotions or airdrops, and can also earn them by participating in certain activities. These NFTs serve as a passport for community members to influence all of the Reptilians' products (web-/mobile apps) and the very life of the company. Community members can vote on the Reptilians' plans and ideas, and propose their own ideas for voting by the community. The NFTs give community members a direct and tangible way to shape the direction of the Reptilians and its products.

  1. We mint NFTs

  2. We give NFTs to the community

  3. The community decides the destiny of our products by the vote supported by NFTs

  4. We implement these decisions to products

How does voting work

In the beginning, we will use a simple chatbot or will make a process manually. But we have a plan to implement voting service on blockchain to automatize it and make a transparent.

In general - it will be pretty simple, democratic, fair, and transparent.

Adios to traditional DAOs:

We want to avoid classic DAO when the community can vote by the number of tokens because we haven't the goal to use tokens as a product, we want to make them as a tool. Because of it, we decide to make the voting process by the wallets that own NFT from the collection connected with the product.

For example: If you have NFT from the GameName collection - you can vote for this game. If you have several NFTs for several collections - you can vote for products or services that are connected with these collections. And for those super special NFTs, like "Philosoraptors" on the Unique network and "Pixeraptors" on the Polygon, you'll have the ability to vote on all aspects of the Reptilians.

Make your voice heard:

For each of our products, we'll have a list of ideas and changes up for a vote. As a holder of NFTs from that product's collection, you'll have the power to vote on each item and help determine the direction of the product. NFTs holders can vote for each point in this list, and the order of the items will change depending on the votes of the users. The next voting always will be for the first item on the list.


Got a great idea? Share it!

All NFT holders will be able to offer their ideas to this list and these ideas are given equal weight in the voting process.

Voting process

It's super simple, really. Just imagine this scenario:

  1. We've got a roadmap for a mobile app, let's call it Reptilian.

  2. We're currently working on some updates for the game.

  3. We're making a list of future changes for Reptilian.

  4. We're sharing it with the community.

  5. Some of our NFT holders may have ideas for changes that they don't see on the list - no problem! They can add their own ideas to the list.

  6. We'll give everyone some time to think about the changes before voting - after all, we're still working on some changes at this point.

  7. The Reptilians will announce the start date of the next voting.

  8. NFT holders will vote on the items on the list.

  9. On the voting start date, the first item on the list will be the next thing we work on.

  10. NFT holders will vote on specific options within the item. For example, it could be "Remove all ads from the game" with options "Yes" or "No". Or "Add a campaign mode to the game" with specific details for the options.

  11. NFTs lock in smart contracts till the end of voting.

  12. Profit, YOU decided what we will do next couple of months!

How do we protect ourselves from ourselves

Yes, there is a lot of danger! We are trying to make a totally new approach to the world. But with that comes the risk of being attacked from all angles.

Some examples of potential attacks include:

  1. Kind of attack of 51%, when somebody has the biggest part of the NFTs and could try to manipulate our voting system and disrupt our vision.

  2. Maybe the community not being ready for the level of autonomy we're giving them and making poor decisions that could harm our company and ideas.

  3. Social engineering tactics that attempt to manipulate individuals into harming the company and community.

  4. And let's be real, the list goes on and on.

We're all about creating a self-governing anarchy-style community that will create something good and change the world in a good way, showing that people can make choices and build the world by themselves.
But, despite this, we need some rules which will bring freedom gradually.

That's why we are creating several rules which will serve multiple purposes:

  1. Save and care for all of the community

  2. Save and care for all of the employees of the Reptilians

  3. Growth of the community and company

  4. Give time for the products to launch

Voting by wallets, not NFTs

We're about keeping it fair and democratic, and that's why we're making sure that voting power is based on the wallet that owns the NFT, not the number of NFTs held. We need to avoid situations when somebody takes the biggest part of the NFTs and wants to make damage to a product or Reptilians. And also it makes voting not fair and not democratic - the voice of this guy will be stronger than the voice of somebody with 1 NFT. It isn't right. We want to make sure that everyone's voice is heard and that's why we're sticking to this approach.

That's why we decide to make voting only by the wallet that owns NFT, doesn't matter how much - a million or one.

Sure, there may be ways for bad actors to hack the system by using millions of wallets - but it will be a really difficult task for this bad guy. And looks like the "game won't be worth the candle" for him.


Veto of the founders

We're all about community-driven decisions, but sometimes things can get some shit. We understand that sometimes the community may make decisions that could harm the Reptilians' potential. That's why, during the early stages of a product's life (about stages in the next block), the founders (4 indie developers who created the Reptilians) have the power to veto a decision made by the community. But don't worry, this veto power comes with responsibility. If the founders use it, they must provide a clear explanation for their decision. No shady business, just making sure we're not making any hasty decisions that could potentially harm the Reptilians or product. This way, the vetoed item goes back to the discussion table and the community (including the founders) can work together to find a solution.

We want to keep it safe all aspects of the Reptilians, and we understand that there will be a possible situation when somebody pushed the community to close a potential idea before it brings results.

But we remember our idea, mission, and goals. That's why we, founders, limit ourselves. These rules will be working only on the first stages of the product's life and after that, we will take away this right from ourselves. Because in the end, this is your company too.


Stages for the products of the Reptilians

And here is the block about stages of the product's life. We're all about taking things step by step and that's why we've got stages for our products. We wanna make sure the community is ready to take on more responsibility as the product grows. We're starting off small and gradually giving more power to the community as we go. The influence of the community will be growth from the 1st stage and decision in the next features and changes, to the 4rt stage when the community can close the product and founders lose veto. Because the start of the product is the time when it can be buried really fast.

Stage 1: NFT holders can vote on changes and propose new ideas for voting.
Stage 2: NFT holders can do the same as stage 1, plus move the product to a "safe zone" (more on that in the next block).
Stage 3: NFT holders can do the same as stage 2, plus close the product down if they want to.
Stage 4: Founders can't use veto anymore.

We'll set a time frame for Stage 1 when we start working on a new product. It could be 6 months, 1 year, or more. We'll decide together with the community. As the product grows and develops, we'll move on to the next stages by voting.


Safe zone for the part of products

We're all about creating a happy and safe work environment for our team and wanna create a kickass workplace for everyone in the Reptilians. So, we've got a "vice versa ban-hammer" in place, and it's used in a fair and just way.

We've got a "Safe Zone" for three of our most important products. This zone is like a fortress that protects these products with all main streams of revenue from any unforeseen events and unexpected drama. It will make life safer for all teammates.

But here's the catch, only three products can be in this zone at a time. So if the community wants to move a new product in, they have to decide which one to kick out. And remember, the founders can use their veto power if needed.


Locking NFTs and responsibility for decisions

We believe in freedom, but we also believe in being responsible for our actions. So, if someone in our community makes a proposal or option inside the proposal that could harm our products or community, we want to make sure they share in that responsibility.

To make sure this happens, any community member can flag the option inside the proposal as potentially harmful, with a reason why. Our team will then review these flags and if they're found to be true, the proposal or vote will be marked as harmful.

Even if these harmful options win a vote, the NFT holders who voted for them won't be left off the hook. Their tokens, which were locked in smart contracts, will be sent to the company and distributed according to our tokenomics rules.

It's all about taking responsibility for our actions and making sure we're moving forward as a community in a safe and responsible way.

These rules help us to keep the balance between the company and community, freedom and responsibilities. And the most important - keep in safe company and community.

Structure

We're all about working together, sharing responsibilities, and making sure all aspects of our product are covered.

But as we grow and bring on more teammates, we plan to structure ourselves in a more traditional way.

  1. We'll create product-specific teams that are responsible for all aspects of their product, like the Reptilian game team.   2. The company won't micromanage the teams, teams will have total freedom to do anything but each team will still have to follow the guidelines outlined in the Reptilians' paper.

  3. We'll also have a Chief level position to coordinate all the teams and ensure the overall well-being of both the Reptilians community and the company.

We're keeping it simple and hope it stays that way.
